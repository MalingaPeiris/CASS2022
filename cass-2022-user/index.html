President

Ms. Malshi Shyamika


Bearing the President’s office for the year 2021/2022, I consider it as a great privilege to present my message for the 36th International CA Students’ Conference 2022. Chartered Accountants Students’ Society (CASS) has come a long way with thirty-five international conferences themed with timely topics that the world, as one Global Village, has faced in different time horizons. This year too, with the theme, “Embrace the transformation. Catch the Wave". CASS attempts to highlight the importance of adapting to change  in trends yet adding up your own style and growing with it.

It is our duty as accountants to be ready to face the dynamic environment. Therefore, the intention of organizing this glamourous event is to create a single platform where the know-how and wisdom of outstanding and skilled professionals are fused and shared.

As well as the last year, we are still facing an unusual challenge amongst the ones that generally barricade our way. It wasn't easy to organize this event with the Covid-19 pandemic. No matter what challenge was in front of us, I believe, we as a team and a family have overcome all these obstacles and made this event a success. The support from the Institute of Chartered Accountants of Sri Lanka is unforgettable. I am grateful for all the prominent personalities who graced this occasion today. Also, my heartfelt thanks to the generous sponsors and to all service providers.

Concluding my message, I would love to thank all the honored guests, the officials of the Institute of Chartered Accountants of Sri Lanka, the sponsors, the delegates from SAFA countries, local participants, and all past as well as current members of CASS for contributing in numerous ways to make this event another successful milestone in CASS history. Enjoy the conference!.

WHO WE ARE 


The Chartered Accountants Students’ Society is the students’ body of the Institute of Chartered Accountants of SriLanka (CA Sri Lanka), which currently boasts of a registered students’ population of above 45,000 and passed finalists of above 5,500.

We were formed in 1981 to provide educational, professional, sports and social activities for the benefit of the students of CA Sri Lanka and also to represent the interests of the students’ population of CA Sri Lanka and currently represent students from 30 Chartered Accountants firm.

Our Society has a history that goes back over two decades, which is of not just events, and achievements that we are proud of, but memories that we take with us as we move on and cherish forever.

OUR VISION

To do, to endeavor, to aspire, to make, to add value for the students.

OUR MISSION


To function as a students' body and to promote further, the interests of the students in the education, literary, sport, social, cultural and any other sphere of activity by co-operating and associating itself with the Institute of Chartered Accountants of Sri Lanka as and when necessary, and to promote social interaction with other student bodies both here and abroad by applying the finance of the society in a manner most conductive to carrying out and promotion of those activities.

INDIA

It was truly an honour to be invited for 34th International  CA Students’ Conference with eight of us  who are humbled to represent the Institute of Chartered  Accountants of India (ICAI). The President of CA Sri Lanka, Mr. Manil Jayasinghe comprehensively covered the topic of the day. The chief guest brought out the importance of the Buddhist way of life by highlighting the six suggestions to adapt to change - the only constant. The conference also gave a platform for a panel discussion - one of the best ways to understand a topic from the viewpoint of another person; exploring unique solutions that would have otherwise remained unexplored. The panel discussion on "Future Ready" revolved around the dire need for accountants to adapt to automation, technology, global best practices, and the pertinent role of emotional intelligence in attaining excellence.


Our enriching experience included a visit to the CA institute where we were welcomed by the CEO of CA Sri Lanka who enlightened future accountants with her rich experience. The visit to Ernst & Young (EY) Sri Lanka gave us insights of leading industry professionals. We were able to visit Sri Lanka’s most visited locations and was also able to observe the perfect culture and the beauty of nature. A farewell party at the breezy beachside location accompanied by loud music and great food is an experience that will always stay with us. Every moment that we spent in Sri Lanka was more cherishing than the previous, however, if we had to pick one thing that stood out the most was the CASS’ hospitality, calm, and amicability. From picking us at the airport at odd hours to making sure that we get pure vegetarian food every time. The whole CASS team left an indelible impact on each of us showing us Sri Lanka’s true hospitality. We express our heartfelt gratitude to CASS and to other delegates for enriching our experience. We extend our invitation to you all to visit India soon! Lots of love from India, stay safe, stay happy, and all the very best for organizing more of such successful events.

PAKISTAN


In the wake of a swift wave of revolution & digitization and risk of losing business relevance for traditional accountants, CASS themed the conference “Count Down – The Future is here. Experienced professionals from different corporates and entrepreneurs were invited to speak on the topic. The speakers gave out an outstanding version of their experience and also extended valuable insights with the audience helping the young accountants to enrich their future. Mr. Michel’s one of the key speaker contrasted concepts about different types of human behaviors and how we, as humans evolve from one to another as age was overwhelming

Two Panel discussion were also part of the agenda, the panelists for 1st discussion were all seasoned professionals, with rich accounting and entrepreneurial/ entrepreneurial backgrounds. The 2nd panel discussion was a student Panel, with Panelists from the delegates of the invited countries and from Sri Lanka. The student’s panel was a very interesting forum. The importance of collaboration and reactiveness for survival in this dynamic environment was perfectly given out. The student’s panel was an exciting panorama where students were able to gain an exclusive knowledge on how future accountants should face challenges and how to be robust, forward looking and dynamic chartered accountants. Overall the conference was a mind-blowing and knowledgeable forum for young accountants who are to lead the world prominently.  

NEPAL

The major highlights of the conference were the enlightenment spread by respected speakers on the future of the workplace of professional accountants. President of CA Sri Lanka provided insights on the need to prepare future accountants with the creative, collaborative, and digital problem-solving skills of the future. Experts expressed their opinion on the disruption to the world of work that digital technologies are likely to bring about skills that will make human resources valuable in the future workplace. Two-panel discussions were held at the conference which focused on ways to prepare future accountants to cope up with possible disorders likely to be welcomed by technological advancement. The conference was lightened by beautiful dances and musical performances by very talented performers.  

We also had the delightful opportunity to experience the beauty of culture, people, and amazing places of Colombo. Meeting people from different countries and collaborating with them has been one of our favorite parts of the conference. We were privileged to visit Ernst and Young, Sri Lanka, and CA Sri Lanka. The amazing event came to an end with a farewell party which we enjoyed to the core. We would like to thank CA Sri Lanka and CA Students' Society of Sri Lanka for making us a part of such a wonderful event and for the amazing and truly unforgettable hospitality.  

BANGLADESH

Two ICAB students Ms. Nimat Zarin, Audit Associate at A. Qasem & Co, Chartered Accountants’ and Mr. Mahdi Hasan, Audit Associate at Rahman Rahman Huq, Chartered Accountants’ represented Bangladesh in the 34th International CA Students’ Conference. The session looked at the future challenges ahead in the career of Chartered Accountants’ from a students’ perspective.  

The four-day tour of the conference not only addressed the issues that future accountants’ will face and need to be prepared of, but also gave an opportunity to explore the unexplainable beauty of Sri Lanka. Among the amazing hospitality of the Students’ Society of CA Sri Lanka, a visit to key tourist attractions in Sri Lanka was remarkable. EY Sri Lanka invited the foreign delegates to visit their firm. The Institute of Chartered Accountants of SriLanka also organized a feedback session with all the students’ and the foreign delegates. The discussion of the conference revolved around the issues that future accountant will adhere and questioning our adoptability to the changes. Challenges from the technological evolvement in the accounting profession to the ethical dilemma arising from the changes were discussed, Members of CA Sri Lanka, local and foreign students were the discussants in the conference. Ms. Nimat Zarin, representing Bangladesh was the Panel Speaker in the Conference and addressed the issues concerning ethical challenges faced in this tech driven business society. CA Students’ Society of Sri Lanka has presented her with a crest for being a part of the students’ panel speaker for #FutureReady discussion.
